L2. Z-transform

2.1 Introduction

We know Laplace transform maps a signal from time domain into sss domain, it operates on continuous signals x(t)x(t)x(t). What about its equivalent for discrete signals?
A discrete time signal is a number sequence with discrete intervals TTT:
[x(0),x(T),x(2T),...]    or    [x0,x1,x2,...][x(0), x(T), x(2T), ...] \;\; \textrm{or} \;\; [x_0,x_1,x_2,...][x(0),x(T),x(2T),...]or[x0​,x1​,x2​,...]
TTT is also known as the sampling period, i.e. the intervals between two signals is the period of time we wait before we take a sample of the continuous signal x(T)x(T)x(T). A standard notation is:
{x(kT)}k≥0    or    {xk}k≥0\{x(kT)\}_{k\geq 0} \;\; \textrm{or} \;\; \{x_k\}_{k\geq 0}{x(kT)}k≥0​or{xk​}k≥0​
Analogous to Laplace transform, the z-transform for {x(kT)}k≥0\{x(kT)\}_{k\geq 0}{x(kT)}k≥0​ is :
∑k=1∞x(kT)e−skT=∑k=1∞x(kT)z−k\sum_{k=1}^\infty x(kT)e^{-skT} = \sum_{k=1}^\infty x(kT)z^{-k}k=1∑∞​x(kT)e−skT=k=1∑∞​x(kT)z−k

Without diving into details, say if we have x(kT)=[3,−3,3,−3,...]{x(kT)}= [3, -3, 3, -3, ...]x(kT)=[3,−3,3,−3,...] and z∣s=0−k=[1,1,1,1...]z_{|s=0}^{-k} = [1,1,1,1...]z∣s=0−k​=[1,1,1,1...], multiplying these together, we get ∑k=1∞x(kT)z∣s=0−k=0\sum_{k=1}^\infty x(kT)z_{|s=0}^{-k}=0∑k=1∞​x(kT)z∣s=0−k​=0, the zero here can be interpreted as a form of correlation between two functions, i.e how much component there is in one function from another, or the response of the system had we excited it with that signal.
